When Water Becomes a Warning

Sustainable Development Goal 6 aims to ensure the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. In our local community, this goal is particularly critical as we face the challenges of urban density and aging drainage systems.

The esteros and canals in our barangay serve as the "veins" of our infrastructure, meant to move rainwater and prevent flooding. However, when these waterways become clogged with silt and waste, they transition from a utility to a health hazard.

This photo essay explores the current state of our local waterways, highlighting the urgent need for improved sanitation and community-wide responsibility to protect our shared environment.

Photographs & Captions
01
Target 6.3 · Water Pollution

The Barrier of Neglect

This photograph captures a narrow urban canal, or estero, tightly hemmed in by stone walls and separated from the public walkway by a chain-link fence. The water is dark and opaque, reflecting a hazy sun that highlights the film of pollutants resting on the surface.

While the fence is intended for safety, it also symbolizes a physical and psychological barrier between the community and its water system. We often treat these canals as "out of sight, out of mind" because they are partitioned off, leading to a lack of communal ownership over their cleanliness.

The "greenery" clinging to the walls isn't a sign of a healthy ecosystem, but rather an indicator of high nutrient loads from greywater runoff. To achieve SDG 6 locally, we must look past the fences and realize that the health of this water directly impacts the health of our air and our people.

SDG 6 · Target 6.3 — Reduce pollution & eliminate dumping into waterways.
02
Sanitation · Public Health

Stagnant Realities

In this close-up view of the waterway, the reality of the sanitation challenge becomes even clearer. Clumps of debris, organic matter, and trapped litter float on the surface of the stagnant, grey water. The concrete banks are stained with marks of fluctuating water levels, showing that during heavy rains, this polluted water rises significantly.

Small plants struggle to grow in the cracks of the concrete — showing nature's resilience — yet the overall state of the water suggests a system that is currently failing to meet sanitary standards.

This image reflects that proper sanitation isn't just about toilets; it's about the safe management of all wastewater and runoff. Achieving SDG 6 requires a "software" update in our community: a change in how we dispose of trash and a more rigorous maintenance schedule from local authorities. Without active intervention, these stagnant pools remain a silent threat to our community's well-being.
